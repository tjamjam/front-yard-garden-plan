The "No-Dig" Philosophy:
Lasagna Gardening

Instead of labor-intensive tilling, we will use sheet mulching. This approach builds fertile, living soil from the top down, saving immense time and resources long-term by eliminating tilling, reducing weeds, and improving soil health.

1

Mow the Lawn

Mow the existing grass as short as possible, leaving the clippings in place.

2

Weed Barrier

Lay down unwaxed cardboard, overlapping edges by 6+ inches to block sunlight and smother the grass.

3

Compost Layer

Add a 2-3 inch layer of high-quality compost to introduce nutrients and beneficial microorganisms.

4

Mulch Layer

Finish with a 3-4 inch layer of organic mulch (shredded bark, wood chips) to retain moisture and suppress new weeds.

Guilds of Berries, Shrubs,
and Living Mulch

This layer fills the space between trees with productive shrubs, edible groundcovers, and perennial herbs that support a self-sustaining ecosystem.

Everbearing Raspberries ('Caroline')

Provides a delicious harvest into late summer and fall. Pruning is simple: cut all canes to the ground in late winter.

American Black Elderberry

A beautiful native shrub. Harvest fragrant white flowers for cordials or let them become berries for jams and syrups.

Black Chokeberry

A hardy native prized for high-antioxidant berries. Offers brilliant red and orange fall foliage.

Wild Strawberry (Living Mulch)

A native perennial that spreads to form a dense, weed-suppressing mat. Produces small, intensely flavorful strawberries.

Creeping Thyme

A tough, drought-tolerant, and walkable groundcover for the sunniest, driest spots. Edible and highly attractive to bees.

Wild Bergamot

A native perennial powerhouse for attracting pollinators. Leaves and flowers can be used for herbal tea.

Low-Maintenance Seasonal Calendar

🌱 Early Spring

Late April - Early May

  • • Implement sheet mulching
  • • Plant bare-root trees & shrubs
  • • Prune raspberry canes to ground

☀️ Early Summer

June

  • • Harvest strawberries
  • • Water new plants weekly if no rain
  • • Hand-pull the few weeds that emerge

🍑 Mid-Summer

July - August

  • • Harvest 'Reliance' peaches
  • • Water only during extended drought
  • • Watch the pollinators

🍎 Fall & Winter

Sep - Mar

  • • Harvest apples and raspberries
  • • Add more mulch if needed
  • • Prune fruit trees during dormancy
